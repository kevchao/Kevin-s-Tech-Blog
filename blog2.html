Future Career

After the first blog post, I had no idea where to go with future posts. So, I thought for this one I would go over my future career plans.

I'll be honest, I struggled to figure out what I would do for my career for years. As a kid, I would say all the usual kid answers when asked what I wanted to be. Things like firefighter, police officer, maybe superhero here and there. Once I got older and more realistic, I managed to at least rule out some jobs that I wouldn't enjoy. Nowadays, I've kind of set my sights on some sort of computer job. I'd be okay with being like a software developer, video game designer, pretty much anything involving making things I enjoy on computers. On that note, I have some friends in my friend group who are also pursuing computer-related careers, so we've joked around about potentially working together to make a game or something. Even if we only made a super basic game to share within our friend groups, I would be fine with that because we actually used our own skills to make something. Who knows, we might expand in the future and go bigger.

If I don't end up developing games or software, I think another career path I could follow might lie in things like photo editing. I'm pretty sure I wouldn't need a degree to do it, since the main idea would be to just show my skill through my own edited pieces. I could be wrong though. I've already taken a Visual Communications (VisCom) and graphic design class back in high school, so with what I learned on my own time, I think I more or less have the basics down. I wouldn't need too much motivation to edit photos either, since I sometimes edit photos from friends. If I don't pursue software or game development I might find myself a professional photoshopper, either as a full time job or just as a hobby, but most likely as a hobby.
